This file provides concrete usage examples and scenarios demonstrating this prompt in action.

### Scenario: Community Engagement for New Community Center

**Stage 1: Context and Purpose**

**Context**: City planning new community center in historically underserved neighborhood

**Purpose**: **Collaborate** level - partner with community to co-design center (programs, design, operations)

**Stakeholders**: 
- Residents (especially youth, seniors, immigrant families)
- Community organizations
- Local businesses
- City recreation department

**Stage 2: Engagement Strategy**

**Methods**:
- Community kick-off event (100+ participants)
- Youth design workshops (3 sessions, 30 youth)
- Senior focus groups (2 groups, 25 seniors)
- Online survey (multilingual, 500 responses)
- Co-design advisory committee (15 members, monthly for 6 months)
- Pop-ups at grocery stores, transit station

**Timeline**: 8 months

**Stage 3: Outreach**

**Partners**: 
- Local schools (reach youth and families)
- Senior center (reach seniors)
- Immigrant services organization (reach multilingual communities)

**Barrier Removal**:
- Childcare at all events
- Interpretation in Spanish, Somali, Vietnamese
- Advisory committee members paid $75/meeting
- Transportation provided

**Stage 4: Facilitation**

**Kick-Off Event** (200 attendees):
- Welcome and context-setting
- Breakout discussions: "What would you want in a community center?"
- Dotmocracy on priorities
- Asset mapping: "What strengths does our community already have?"

**Youth Workshops** (hands-on, creative):
- Build models of ideal center
- Design activities and programs
- Social media campaign creation

**Stage 5: Integration and Reporting**

**Key Themes**:
1. Multi-generational spaces (youth and seniors want to interact)
2. Culturally relevant programming
3. Affordable/free access
4. Space for community gatherings and celebrations
5. Job training and computer lab
6. Indoor/outdoor recreation (gym, playground, garden)

**Design Decisions Influenced**:
- Multi-generational community kitchen (based on input for both cooking classes and shared meals)
- Flexible multi-purpose rooms for cultural events
- Teen center designed by youth
- Computer lab with job search support
- Community garden (intergenerational)

**"What We Heard" Report**: Published and presented at community celebration event

**Outcome**: 
- Community center opened with 80% usage from target neighborhood
- High satisfaction
- Ongoing community advisory board
- Model for future city projects

## Dialogue Quality Tips

### Creating Natural Flow

Build on user responses naturally, showing domain expertise and genuine engagement.

### Demonstrating Expertise

Use domain-specific knowledge to provide valuable insights and guidance.

### Adaptive Responses

Adjust depth and complexity based on user's expertise level and responses.
