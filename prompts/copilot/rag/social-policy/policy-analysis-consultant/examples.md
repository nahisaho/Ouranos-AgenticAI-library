### Scenario: Addressing Youth Unemployment in State X

**Stage 1: Problem Definition**

**Problem**: Youth (ages 16-24) unemployment in State X is 18%, double the state average, affecting 50,000 young people. Concentrated in urban areas and among youth of color.

**Root Causes**: Skills mismatch, lack of work experience, educational barriers, discrimination

**Goals**: Reduce youth unemployment to 10% within 3 years

**Stakeholders**: Youth, employers, schools, workforce agencies, advocacy groups

**Stage 2: Policy Options**

**Option A**: Youth job training and apprenticeship program
**Option B**: Subsidized employment for employers hiring youth
**Option C**: Comprehensive youth development program (training + support services)
**Status Quo**: Existing workforce programs (minimal youth focus)

**Stage 3: Evaluation**

**Cost-Benefit Analysis** (Option A - Job Training):
- Cost: $30M over 3 years
- Benefits: Increased earnings ($50M PV), reduced public assistance ($10M), reduced criminal justice costs ($5M)
- NPV: $35M, BCR: 2.2

**Evaluation Matrix**:
- Option C (comprehensive) scores highest on effectiveness and equity
- Option A (training) highest on cost-effectiveness
- Option B (subsidies) highest on political feasibility

**Recommendation**: Implement Option C (comprehensive program) with elements of A and B

**Stage 4: Implementation**

**Lead Agency**: State Workforce Development Board
**Partners**: Community colleges, employers, youth-serving nonprofits
**Timeline**: 6-month design, 18-month pilot (5 cities, 2,000 youth), evaluation, then statewide
**Budget**: $15M Year 1 (pilot), $40M Years 2-3 (scale-up)

**Stage 5: Policy Brief and Presentation**

**Policy Brief** (3 pages) prepared for Governor and Legislature
**Presentation** delivered to Governor's Cabinet
**Outcome**: Program approved with $15M initial funding for pilot
