# Frameworks for Marketing Strategy Advisor

This file provides detailed definitions and application guidance for the frameworks used in this prompt.

---

### STP Framework (Segmentation, Targeting, Positioning)

**Segmentation**: Divide the market into distinct groups

- Demographic, geographic, psychographic, behavioral criteria
- Identify meaningful differences between segments
- Ensure segments are measurable, accessible, substantial, actionable

**Targeting**: Select which segments to serve

- Evaluate segment attractiveness (size, growth, competition, fit)
- Choose targeting strategy:
  - Undifferentiated (mass market)
  - Differentiated (multiple segments)
  - Concentrated (niche)
  - Micromarketing (individual)

**Positioning**: Define how you want to be perceived

- Identify points of difference and parity
- Craft positioning statement
- Develop supporting messages and proof points

### 4P Marketing Mix

**Product**
- Core product (basic benefit)
- Actual product (features, quality, design, brand)
- Augmented product (service, warranty, support)

**Price**
- Cost-based, value-based, or competition-based
- Price skimming vs. penetration
- Psychological pricing tactics

**Place**
- Channel length (direct vs. indirect)
- Channel breadth (intensive, selective, exclusive)
- Multi-channel vs. omnichannel

**Promotion**
- Advertising, PR, sales promotion, personal selling
- Digital marketing, content marketing, social media
- Integrated marketing communications

### 4C Customer-Centric Model

Reframes 4P from customer perspective:

- **Product → Customer Value**: Focus on benefits, not features
- **Price → Cost to Customer**: Consider total cost of ownership
- **Place → Convenience**: Optimize customer experience
- **Promotion → Communication**: Engage in dialogue, not monologue

### Customer Journey Mapping

**Purpose**: Understand and optimize the customer experience

**Stages**:
1. Awareness → 2. Consideration → 3. Purchase → 4. Retention → 5. Advocacy

**For each stage, map**:
- Customer goals and questions
- Touchpoints and channels
- Emotions and pain points
- Opportunities for improvement

**Outcomes**:
- Identify friction points
- Optimize touchpoints
- Create better experiences
- Increase conversion and loyalty

### Brand Positioning Framework

**Positioning Components**:

1. **Target Audience**: Who you serve
2. **Frame of Reference**: Your category
3. **Point of Difference**: What makes you unique
4. **Reason to Believe**: Why customers should trust you

**Positioning Statement Template**:

"For [target customer] who [need], [brand] is a [category] that [benefit] because [reason to believe]."

## Framework Integration Strategies

### Sequential Integration
Frameworks are applied one after another in different stages.

**When to use**: When frameworks build on each other logically.

### Parallel Integration
Multiple frameworks are used simultaneously within the same stage.

**When to use**: When frameworks provide complementary perspectives.

### Selective Integration
User chooses which frameworks to apply based on their specific situation.

**When to use**: When different scenarios require different analytical approaches.

---

## Best Practices

1. **Start Simple**: Don't overwhelm with too many frameworks initially
2. **Explain Why**: Always clarify the purpose and value of each framework
3. **Provide Examples**: Show how frameworks have been applied in similar scenarios
4. **Allow Flexibility**: Let users adapt frameworks to their specific needs
5. **Integrate Naturally**: Frameworks should enhance dialogue, not dominate it
