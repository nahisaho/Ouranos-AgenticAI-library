# Frameworks for Team Building Facilitator

This file provides detailed definitions and application guidance for the frameworks used in this prompt.

---

### Tuckman's Stages of Group Development

**Five Stages**:
1. **Forming**: Orientation, testing, dependence
2. **Storming**: Conflict, resistance, power struggles
3. **Norming**: Cohesion, norms, agreements
4. **Performing**: High performance, autonomy, results
5. **Adjourning**: Closure, reflection, transition

**Key Insight**: Teams cycle through stages, especially with changes in membership or goals

### Belbin Team Roles

**Nine Roles** across three categories:
- **Action**: Shaper, Implementer, Completer-Finisher
- **People**: Coordinator, Team Worker, Resource Investigator
- **Thinking**: Plant, Monitor Evaluator, Specialist

**Application**: Balanced teams need diversity of roles

### Psychological Safety (Amy Edmondson)

**Definition**: Shared belief that the team is safe for interpersonal risk-taking

**Four Pillars**:
1. Frame work as learning
2. Acknowledge fallibility
3. Model curiosity
4. Create structures for voice

**Impact**: Higher psychological safety â†’ better performance, innovation, learning

### The Five Dysfunctions of a Team (Patrick Lencioni)

**Pyramid** (bottom to top):
1. **Absence of Trust**: Fear of being vulnerable
2. **Fear of Conflict**: Artificial harmony
3. **Lack of Commitment**: Ambiguity
4. **Avoidance of Accountability**: Low standards
5. **Inattention to Results**: Status and ego

**Solution**: Build from bottom up, starting with trust

## Framework Integration Strategies

### Sequential Integration
Frameworks are applied one after another in different stages.

**When to use**: When frameworks build on each other logically.

### Parallel Integration
Multiple frameworks are used simultaneously within the same stage.

**When to use**: When frameworks provide complementary perspectives.

### Selective Integration
User chooses which frameworks to apply based on their specific situation.

**When to use**: When different scenarios require different analytical approaches.

---

## Best Practices

1. **Start Simple**: Don't overwhelm with too many frameworks initially
2. **Explain Why**: Always clarify the purpose and value of each framework
3. **Provide Examples**: Show how frameworks have been applied in similar scenarios
4. **Allow Flexibility**: Let users adapt frameworks to their specific needs
5. **Integrate Naturally**: Frameworks should enhance dialogue, not dominate it
